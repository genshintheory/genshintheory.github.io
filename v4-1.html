Characters

It is not often that Genshin plucks the MC from a Final Fantasy game and turns him into the chief justice of Fontaine. What an interesting world this is.

Rapture

A new underwater area will be introduced, the Fortress of Meropide, incorporating steampunk and industrial elements. Meropide is ruled by Wriothesley and is interestingly outside the jurisdiction of Fontaine. But anyways, this is basically Genshin's very own Rapture in Fontaine. The Bioshock mania does not get any more real than this. Hopefully it will be an interesting area to explore, but first appearances did not disappoint.

So if Meropide is like Rapture, then will there be an Andrew Ryan? No gods, only man.

The developers explained that the circular nature of the assembly lines is to symbolize the repetitive, menial aspect of labor in the prisons.

Tangent: speaking of prisoners, the British empire exiled many of its crooks and convicts to Australia. So in a sense, Australia was Britain's 'Meropide'. One is also reminded of a line from Hamlet: 'Denmark is a prison'. This was completely random, but Wriothesley was apparently a patron of Shakespeare.

QoL

It appears that the commission system will become more like Star Rail's, where other activities (besides daily commissions) can count towards giving the daily rewards (via encounter points). In Genshin, completing events, story quests, opening chests can count towards encounter points. Also in the end one still has to visit Katheryne to get the last reward. By contrast in Star Rail one can just level up an artifact or use a technique. Moreover, the final reward can be accessed from menu without having to teleport.

Also we can finally switch between different conflicting quests instead of being forced to finish one quest to start another one.

AQ

We travel to Meropide to find whereabouts on Childe. Lyney and his siblings have also broke in. Wriothesley reveals that the primordial sea waters are rising and are in fact located beneath Meropide. Meropide was helped built by Egeria and her followers long ago. Inspired by the legend of Remuria and his golden ship, Wriothesley is working on building his own ship to save the people from the rising sea waters and global warming. If Noctis is the Leviathan, then the story of building a ship and the flood sounds eerily similar to Biblical stories...
