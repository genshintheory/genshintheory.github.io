QoL

It appears that the commission system will become more like Star Rail's, where other activities (besides daily commissions) can count towards giving the daily rewards (via encounter points). In Genshin, completing events, story quests, opening chests can count towards encounter points. Also in the end one still has to visit Katheryne to get the last reward. By contrast in Star Rail one can just level up an artifact or use a technique. Moreover, the final reward can be accessed from menu without having to teleport.

Also we can finally switch between different conflicting quests instead of being forced to finish one quest to start another one.

Overall this is a step in the right direction. It does save time as completing events can reward points. The next step would be to make completing domains, leveling artifacts, synthesizing/cooking stuff award points. Moreover, the ability to claim the bonus award from the handbook (rather than visit the Adventurer's Guild) as in Star Rail would make this more like quality of life.

Unfinished Comedy

In this WQ, we meet Caterpillar (Cater) and Lanoire and help break them out of Meropide. Cater was sent to Meropide 400 years ago. On the surface, he looks like a kid with blackened arms and legs. But his true form is an anemo hilichurl rogue. His master, Narzissenkreuz, performed some experiments at the Ordo to make him look like a person, while also giving him intelligence. If only Narzissenkreuz could have done the same for Chlothar Alberich's kid Caribert...

A guard named Estienne helps Cater and Lanoire escape. Lanoire returns to her family, her grandfather Earnshaw. Lanoire's mother is Agnes.

Cater has some interesting dialogue, saying there are 'mutually exclusive' and conflicting elements in the nature of his existence. He says sometimes he is a bloomguard (pari) and other times a dragon. To interpret this, we will dive a bit more deeply into the lore of Genshin.

In Teyvat, there are three realms: light realm, void realm, and the human realm. The light realm or vishap realm is the elemental realm of the vishaps and dragons. The hydro dragon Noctis is of this realm and is able to harness the power of hydro without a vision. The void realm is associated with the abyss. The human realm is created by the Primordial One and is associated with the power of heaven and celestia. The bloomguards, khvarena, and pari are associated with the power of heaven too.

Some have likened the vishap realm to the elemental system and the void and human realms to the traditional dark and light (holy) elements in games.

There is a whole fan theory on how the traveler is able to harness elemental power and how that connects with dragon pearls, allogenes, gnosis. Moreover, this connects to the lore of King Deshret, the Dragon King Nibelung, and the Primordial One. (As of 4.1 it is unconfirmed whether the Primordial One is the Heavenly Principles or whether they are different, but both are related to Celestia, so it could be safe to say that Nibelung was fighting Celestia.) This will be written more about later.

As discussed in Rene's lore, things like abyssal energy (void realm) and heavenly energy (human realm) possess a higher 'authority' than the elements of the dragon realm. It is said that the abyssal energy is able to possess a 'will', and Rene was also able to cure Jakob by using abyssal energy.

Long ago, the Primordial One fought a war against the seven dragon sovereigns and won. The Primordial One then took a portion of the dragons' elemental authority, creating gnosis that would later be bestowed upon the gods. So dragons such as Noctis do not have the power they once had ('Dragon Authority') as some of that authority was given to archons such as Furina. Noctis even says that to regain his full power, Furina (or whoever has the hydro gnosis) would need to return that 'authority' to him.

Whether this lost dragon authority has anything to do with the abyss and heaven possessing a higher 'authority' over the elements remains to be seen.

The main point is that when Cater says he sometimes identifies with a bloomguard (human realm) and dragon (light realm), it seems to suggest that his existence could be related to the human realm of Celestia and the elemental realm of dragons. Cater even refers to these different components as 'mutually exclusive', being 'pure' but also 'alien' to each other, similar to how the three realms are exclusive to each other in Teyvat. It may be that his master Narzissenkreuz harnessed one of these powers (Celestia, elemental, or even abyssal) to transform Cater into human form. After all, his master did use the Holy Blade of Narzissenkreuz to achieve the transmutation.

Hilichurls were once humans that were cursed by Celestia, thereby transforming them into monsters. This happened to non-pure Khaenriah people such as Chlothar's son. Recall Caribert received the power of the abyss from a sinner's statue, and this caused Caribert to regain some human speech and intelligence, eerily similar to how Cater gained speech and intelligence through his master. This incident inspired Chlothar (Eide) to found the Abyss Order as he believed the power of the abyss was needed to overthrow Celestia, the ones who had cursed his son in the first place.

It seems the power of these realms (abyss, celestia, elemental energy) can give hilichurls human intelligence and speech, as is demonstrated via Caribert (and possibly even Cater, though this is unconfirmed). In other words it takes the power of these realms to combat the curse of Celestia.

Throughout the history of Teyvat, it seems Chlothar was not the only one who realized that abyssal power was needed to combat the holy realm of Celestia. It appears that the traveler's sibling, after journeying through Teyvat and gathering the seven elemental authorities, may have also realized that the elements (dragon authority) were not enough and that they needed a higher authority (the abyss) to fight Celestia. Hence, the traveler's twin became the prince/princess of the abyss. This would make sense if the abyssal and human realms had a higher authority than the dragon elemental realm. Moreover, some have theorized that the dragon king Nibelung (and maybe even Deshret) also combined the powers of the abyss and elements to combat Celestia. It is known that Nibelung used forbidden knowledge to fight Celestia. Forbidden knowledge is what causes the withering, eleazar, and oozing filth. It is not known if forbidden knowledge has any connection to the abyss or other realms. The Heavenly Principles had to cast down divine nails from Celestia to purify the world of this forbidden knowledge.

Forbidden knowledge is known to have caused corrupting and destructive effects on living beings. On a sidenote, the Arkhium found in Fontaine has also been described as having a similar negative effect on people (as well as Primordial Water to another extent). However, as of 4.1 it is not known if Arkhe and Primordial Water are connected at all to the power of the three realms, nevermind forbidden knowledge. They could all just be separate things.

Much of the above remains to be confirmed/disproven and is still theory (albeit at least backed by textual evidence). Some have remarked how the first 2-3 years of Genshin did not really have much lore on things like the history of Teyvat, the Abyss, Descenders, Realms, the Primordial One, Dragons, etc. But as we move further along in the Traveler's journey, inevitably more key story points will reveal themselves, and perhaps Genshin Impact will become more like lore impact.

Life Bond

Fontaine introduced a new mechanic called life bond via the Fatui Operatives and some weapons. Life Bond is almost similar to the Prana effect in Star Rail in the sense that it prevents hp from going above a certain amount. However, in Star Rail the prana can be cleared via debuffing skills, whereas Life Bond can be cleared by excess healing. Apparently one kind of Life Bond effect can last 10 minutes (really long) if not cleared through healing.

The Operatives also apply a corrosion effect in addition to the life bond. What this means is that you want to bring a healer for these guys. Interestingly, the Fontaine characters that can self-heal a ton such as Lyney and Noctis can be effective at clearing life bonds. Imagine a spiral abyss that puts operatives, riftwolves, and the serpent knights together in a floor.

So Fontaine is really cementing itself as the land of hydro and HP, whether it involves HP drain, healing, life bonds, etc. This is the path of destruction.
