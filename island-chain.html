For the first 3 years of Genshin, Xinyan was the 4 star with the highest base atk (with Albedo, Morax a bit higher). In 4.0, Freminet became the first 4 star to break through the 'first island chain'.

Island chains (clusters of characters with same base stat):

  • 1st island chain Albedo, Morax: the alchemist/scientist and his financer
  • 2nd island chain Mona, Qiqi: standard duo
  • 3rd island chain Nahida, Ayato
  • 4th island chain Wriothesley, Klee: standard Lee
  • 5th island chain Cyno, Lyney: Hamilton duo, political founders
  • 6th island chain Yoimiya, Keqing: the Qing ladies
  • 7th island chain Diluc, Ganyu: only after observing Genshin's development for the first 3-4 years does one realize how strong Diluc is canonically in the lore. If Ganyu is akin to a minor deity in Liyue, then Diluc's strength is nearly on par with that of gods.
  • 8th island chain Eula, Ayaka: the superhumans of Teyvat, what a cute pair, literally stronger than archons (Raiden)

As a rule of thumb, generally speaking once you start reaching Tartaglia's level in strength do you begin to have 'high' attack (above 300). The range between Childe and Alhaitham can be called 'high'. The range between Cyno/Lyney to Scaramouche can be called 'pretty high'. And things around Diluc/Ganyu level and above are 'very high' and on the level of gods/deities.

If there is anything the year of Sumeru showed, it is that being placed above Diluc/Ganyu is a privilege, not a right. Gone are the days of Inazuma where literally every new character was higher than Diluc/Ganyu (Eula, Ayaka, Raiden, Yae). On the other hand, Sumeru did see some novel revolutions in the hp dimension with Dehya and Nilou.
