Social mobility

Social mobility is a subject often studied. The data can be presented in a 2d mobility table, where the rows represent the previous generation's status (occupation, rank, income, etc.) and the columns represent the next generation's status. The diagonal cells mean that the next generation has the same status as the previous. That is, it refers to social immobility, no progress. Sometimes there may even be downward mobility.

Social mobility has been studied across different societies, countries, time frames from Victorian era to present and even further back. But we can also study social mobility in the context of video game characters such as those in Honkai Star Rail. Suppose the socioeconomic status (SES) of an HSR character is approximated by their base atk (can also study base def, hp but let's focus on atk for now). Then we have an interesting case study: in 1.0 Jing Yuan was released, and then a year later Huangquan was released. This is an example of social immobility, and in fact may even be seen as downward mobility when looking at Ratio.

In other words, the kind of corporate culture that HSR promotes is one where only characters like Fu Xuan or Ratio experience rapid upward mobility, whereas characters like Jing Yuan, Huangquan, Black Swan, Topaz experience no mobility or downward mobility. Such a virtual society / system seems clearly designed to oppress certain characters.
