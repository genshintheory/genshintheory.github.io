Art

The 4.0 sp stated that the Genshin art team based Fontaine's geography on Europe and they tried to distinguish Fontaine from Mondstadt, given the two are close to each other. They mentioned they were inspired by baroque era and art deco designs. In particular, they based some of the designs off of the Alps.

Fontaine is surrounded by waterfalls. One of the designers mentioned that rendering water is a challenge in the industry.

Diving

The diving and underwater swimming is done right in Genshin. In too many other games, swimming underwater is a slow and tedious process. But Genshin actually makes diving quite fast, even faster than swimming above water. So the diving experience is pretty smooth so far. And it is convenient that one can switch characters/team underwater too. Apparently, there is no need to breath as there is no oxygen meter as per the livestream.

Will we be able to dive underwater in other areas of the map? Like in the waters around the Alcor, Liyue, or Inazuma?

If there was one thing some Bioshock fans wanted in Rapture: it was the ability to swim and dive underwater. Genshin did a good job bringing this mechanic to the game.

The path of destruction

The Chiming domain (Pale Forgotten) gives buffs based on hp changes: nice domain for Xiao. Fontaine is starting to look like Xiao land with all the hp gimmicks: the land of destruction. Perhaps Xiao got his 'western education' in Fontaine, learning the art of the destruction dao.

Archon quest (Chapter 4)

Act I and Act II are some of the most interesting archon quests ever written in Genshin's history. Whether it is good or bad depends on the performance of future versions, but if anything it was interesting. The ace attorney court scenes involving making refutations and compiling evidence was one thing that made it interesting. The mystery and detective work as well as the general story-telling also made it interesting. Interesting just means that the first three chapters of Genshin did not capture the attention as much as Chapter 4.

However, there will be a penalty. The people who chose the names of the NPCs in the various areas of Fontaine as well as the people who wrote the story on Marcel know exactly what they did. And they know why either version 4.0 individually or version 4 as a whole will be penalized for these choices. How big the penalty is depends on how the rest of version 4 goes, including future archon quests. But it seems that certain people do not want version 4 to be as good as or better than version 1. There is even some doubt that version 4 will be able to surpass versions 2 or 3.

Here we give a reminder: the story quests are one of the most important factors in deciding how good a version is. A major screwup in the story can end up killing an entire version, no matter what else happens in that version.

So what happened? During Lyney's magic show, Cowell attempts to dissolve a lady (a thief) using primordial water. However, the lady fights back and stuffs Cowell into a box. Cowell then gets crushed to death by a water tank. It is discovered that Cowell colluded with Vaughn to dissolve women with primordial water. Vaughn was found guilty and got dissolved by water. The lady is also caught and found guilty of theft. The death of Cowell was fully deserved, and this part of the story was brilliantly written.

Meanwhile, Lyney reveals that during his magic trick, he tried to enter the room containing the Oratrice to find out how it works and why it seems to possess a consciousness. However, he hears a voice in the room.

Later it is found out that Marcel was behind the disappearance case and how many people got dissolved by primordial water. He lost his lover as she dissolved in primordial water. He then entered the Sinthe business, experimenting with primordial water in an attempt to find a way to bring back his lover. Primordial water can dissolve people of Fontaine but not others. It seems after people are dissolved they turn into Oceanids and flow to the Fountaine of Lucine, where all water in Fontaine congregates.

Navia's father Callas got caught up in the Sinthe business. He wanted to end it as Sinthe, like an opioid, had a deteriorating effect on people. However, Callas ended up getting implicated in a murder / assassination case. Charged with murder, he decided to defend his honor in a duel, where he was killed by Clorinde. Callas, afflicted with an illness, did not have many years to live left anyways, so he chose to die. Thereafter, he became known as Callas the Unfaithful.

The scene with Marcel getting drowned at the end is reminiscent of how Booker DeWitt (Comstock) is drowned by all the copies of Elizabeth in Bioshock Infinite. Booker (Comstock) turns out to be the father of Elizabeth in a parallel multiverse. A morbid scene, but it was quite the surprise that Genshin imitated that part of Bioshock as well, with all the other jubilant fanfare about the Steambird, Victorian era steampunk, cities underwater like Rapture, flying high in the sky lke Columbia.

Bioshock Infinite also got a lot more into quantum physics, spacetime tears, and multiverse theories than most other games. In the Bioshock games, the MC also gets to use various different elemental powers via plasmids/vigors (besides using different kinds of firearms and guns). It would be nice if the Genshin MC could be as powerful.
