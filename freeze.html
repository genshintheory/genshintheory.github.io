Freeze teams are a type of team based on immobilizing enemies and taking advantage of the crit rate buff from the blizzard set and cryo resonance. They are usually composed of two cryo characters, one hydro, and one anemo (for vv/swirl/cc, etc).

Freeze teams have two main drawbacks: 1) not every enemy can be frozen and 2) they usually do not make use of Bennett's broken atk buff, which results in a penalty in their team dps.

Oftentimes when freeze teams do work, it basically becomes an Anemo cc/vv showcase.

Team structure

Two cryo characters are usually used for cryo resonance +15% crit rate against enemies affected by cryo. Typical candidates include: Shenhe, Chongyun, Rosaria, Ganyu, Kaeya, Ayaka, Diona, Qiqi, etc. Usually one has a cryo dps with a cryo support. Cryo dps include Ganyu, Ayaka. Supports include Shenhe, Chongyun, Diona, Qiqi. Sub-dps include Rosaria, Kaeya. Note Eula while Cryo is usually used on physical teams. Eula's claymore hits will shatter frozen targets. It can be used in a type of shatter composition, but here we focus more on keeping enemies frozen.

Usually the cryo dps will run the 4p blizzard strayer set for +40% crit rate against frozen targets. So in total, the cryo dps can get +55% cr from cryo resonance and 4p blizzard. As people point out, this allows one to stack more crit damage on the cryo dps.

A hydro character is needed for the frozen reaction. Candidates include: Mona, Yelan, Kokomi, Xingqiu, and in some cases Childe, Barbara, etc. Note on Mona: her Q can get a so-called 'omen extension' on frozen targets, allowing her large dmg bonus buff to last longer. This usually makes Mona one of the best choices for freeze teams in terms of dmg bonus buffing. Second note: since Yelan scales with HP, and freeze teams usually do not run Bennett, that can make Yelan a more preferable choice than Xingqiu for freeze teams.

Lastly an anemo character is used for vv shred, cc, swirl, etc. The best candidates include: Venti (cc), Jean (healing), Kazuha (dmg bonus buff), Sucrose (EM buff). These anemo characters are basically universally good for most teams in the game. Not much else needs to be said on their talents.

It is anticipated that Heizou will also be good for freeze teams.

Calculations

Most freeze teams will not have Bennett, so divide team dps by 1.5 (a penalty of 33%). From the anemo vv character, RES is reduced by -40% giving a 1.27 increase in dps. Cryo resonance + blizzard strayer gives +55% cr. Expected damage is proportional to $$x = 1+rd$$ With 55% more cr the new damage becomes $$x' = 1+rd + .55d$$ The damage increase is $$R = x'/x = 1 + \frac{.55d}{1+rd}$$ Note a Bennett team can have the atk buff plus melt (giving roughly a 1.5*2 = 3 times multiplier). It is unclear how freeze teams can achieve a similar 3 times multiplier without Bennett to compensate. To maximize R above, note the base crit rate is usually \(r=.05.\) Only for \(d \approx 5 \) or more can the damage increase R reach 3 or more. This corresponds to 500% crit damage, which is basically impossible to achieve in practice. Even with 300% crit damage, R can only give around 1.43 times more damage. So the blizzard set fails to outdamage Bennett melt teams.

So why use freeze teams at all? The real benefit of freeze teams (when enemies can be frozen) is that when the enemy is immobilized, it makes it easier for all attacks to hit. If the enemy jumps around all over the place and is not frozen, that might result in some inefficiencies. Some attacks may miss and so on. Freezing the enemy makes efficient gamemplay ('playing optimally') easier to achieve. But if you're already playing optimally in the first place, melt teams can usually give higher dps.
