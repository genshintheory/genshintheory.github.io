Chihayaburu damage and EM considerations

We assume level 10 E and level 10 plunging attack with 61.6% anemo dmg bonus. Note one of his passives will deal an additional 200% of ATK of the absorbed element. And another passive can give him .04% dmg bonus per EM point for the swirled element.

A level 90 Kazuha will innately have 115.2 EM. With a level 90 Freedom Sworn he can get 198 more EM. This gives around 300 EM at least. Most players may not have Freedom Sworn but maybe Iron Sting or even Sac Sword. The total amount of EM even varies on build and artifacts (whether stacking EM or building atk). We will make a very rough approximation and assume Kazuha can get 5% dmg bonus of the swirled element from the passive (has a little more than 120 EM). This is more like a lower bound while accounting for possible EM on substats, without forcing Kazuha to have an EM sword.

The dps of a press E is then $$x_{E1} = ((345.6+404.02)*1.616 + 200*1.05)/6 = 236.897653$$

The dps of a hold E is $$x_{E2} = ((469.44+404.02)*1.616 + 200*1.05)/9 = 180.1679289$$ Although the scaling is higher for hold E, the cd is longer, resulting in the difference above. Holding E gives less dps, so why would you ever use it? One, hold E has a much wider aoe for both damage and cc. Two, if you have sac sword or C1 and can reset the E, this circumvents the downside of longer cd.

We ignore swirl damage and assume Kazuha is built more on ATK (to capitalize from the high scalings in his kit). Considering how Kazuha scales with EM, the swirl damage could be significant though. We assume a high plunge each time and no other modifiers such as Childe's passive increasing NA talent by 1 (though in practice Childe's passive can matter as Kazuha is often used on Childe teams). Note Kazuha has the same plunging scaling as Xiao, perhaps to symbolize the friendship between their Japanese voice actors and the fact they are both Anemo plungers.

Burst damage

The burst consists of an initial slash followed by 5 hits. Each hit consists of two parts: anemo damage plus additional elemental damage if an autumn element (pyro/elector/hydro/cryo) is absorbed. The cd is 15s. The dps at level 10 is $$x_Q = ((472.32 + 5*216)*1.616 + 5*64.8*1.05)/15 = 189.916608$$ Note any absorbed elemental dmg will also get a buff from Kazuha's passive and VV shred (-40% res).

The total dps from press E and Q is $$x = x_{E1} + x_Q = 370.0845369$$ This dps is higher than Ayaka's CA dps. Again this is ignoring swirl damage and assumes Kazuha has a lower amount of EM. With more EM and factoring in swirl, the dps can increase (but not drastically). A C6 Xiangling still does more damage than above, but with more EM, a C0 Kazuha may be able to reach C6 Xiangling's level. Based on these calculations, adding Kazuha to support the team is like adding another dps unit. Kazuha's role is more sub-dps, though he can buff your team with VV, dmg bonus, and CC.

Kazuha, Sucrose, Venti

Kazuha, Sucrose, Venti are all Anemo supports and have similar functions. They are also among the best supports in the game. So how do they compare with each other?

Kazuha will do more damage as a sub-DPS through his E and Q than Sucrose. But in terms of pure support/buffing potential, a C0 Kazuha is similar to a C6 Sucrose. Kazuha can provide 30-40% dmg bonus on swirl, which is more than Sucrose C6 through Q, and Kazuha arguably has more convenient and easier-to-use CC than Sucrose. Advantage of C6 Sucrose over C0 Kazuha in terms of buffing is giving EM and ATK boost through TTDS. Moreover, Sucrose is generally a better battery (3 charges on E), which is important for say Xiao. However C2 Kazuha will powercreep C6 Sucrose in most of these aspects (C1 gives another use of E after Q, C2 gives EM).

As for the Anemo Archon, Venti simply has stronger and bigger AoE CC than Kazuha. Moreover when it comes to constellations Venti is better at reducing Anemo RES (important for Xiao), which is something Kazuha cannot do. All of them can do VV shred. However, Kazuha and Sucrose powercreep Venti in the following: giving damage bonus and giving EM. They can buff reaction teams more than Venti. But Venti remains the best buffer for Anemo damage in the game (with constellations, reducing Anemo RES), and Venti remains the best CC unit in the game.

And as far as meta is concerned, one really uses Venti for his broken CC (which nobody can powercreep) or (if they have C2/C6) his Anemo RES reduction, which Xiao loves.
