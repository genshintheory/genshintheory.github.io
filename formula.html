Resistance

If you assume the enemy has 10% RES to physical and all elements:

  • This gives a 0.9 times multiplier to incoming damage.
  • Viridescent Venerer -40% RES (for Hydro, Pyro, Electro, Cryo) gives -30% RES or a 1.15 multiplier to incoming damage. So VV increases damage by times. If the enemy has more than 10% base RES, this number will be greater. If they have less than 10% base RES, this number will be smaller.
    • Superconduct also lowers Physical RES by 40% so it gives a similar damage increase.
  • Zhongli shield -20% all RES gives -10% RES or a 1.05 multiplier. So his shield increases damage by \( 1.05/.9\approx 1.167 \) times.

Bennett Buff

Notes on Bennett's burst (Fantastic Voyage) ATK buff:

  • Scales off Bennett's base ATK (weapon ATK + character base ATK without artifacts)[1]
  • C1 gives an extra 20% of his base ATK (additive with the ATK Bonus ratio)
  • C5 increases level by 3
  • Has 60 energy cost and 12 second duration with 15 second cooldown.

Suppose Bennett's Base ATK is a (for a well-invested Bennett, this can be within the range 600-900; for example a Level 90 Bennett with a Level 90 Skyward Blade will have around Base ATK; this can be increased further if one uses an Aquila Favonia instead).

If one has a C5 Bennett with level 12 Q, the buff gives

\( {\text{Bennett Buff}}=(1.12+.2)a=1.32a \)

extra ATK to party members within Bennett's field. For a well-invested Bennett, it can usually give at least 1000 extra ATK to characters.

If a character has 2000 ATK initially, then Bennett's buff can increase it to 3000 ATK (increasing damage by 1.5 times).

Actual numbers may be more or less, but this is just to give a ballpark estimate for how much Bennett can increase damage. For some characters who gain other ATK boosts, Bennett's buff may not work as effectively or may hit diminishing returns.

Thrilling Tales

An R5 Thrilling Tales (TTDS) will increase ATK by 48% (for the new character swapped in). This is additive with other sources of ATK%. Suppose one's DPS character has an ATK% sands (giving 46.6 ATK%) and no other sources of ATK% or flat ATK (none on artifact substats, character ascensions). Then TTDS will increase damage by times. If one has additional ATK% from other sources then this boost will be smaller.

The buff will last 10 seconds and can be procced once every 20 seconds.

If one combines TTDS with Bennett's buff, then Bennett's ATK boost is added after the TTDS buff is factored in. That is, for the calculation, let A be the target character's base ATK. Then the buffed attack is:

\( A(1+{\text{ATK }}\%+{\text{TTDS Buff}})+{\text{Flat ATK}}+{\text{Bennett Buff}}} \)

Example: for a character with say 2000 ATK and ATK% sands and a well-invested Bennett, combining TTDS with Bennett (use Bennett Q, swap to TTDS character, swap to DPS) can give around 3600 ATK (actual number in game may be more or less), increasing damage by around 1.8 times.

The total damage gain will be the damage gain from TTDS plus the damage gain of Bennett's ATK boost:

$$\begin{aligned}{\text{ATK Increase Ratio}}={\frac {\text{Buffed ATK}}{\text{Initial ATK}}}&={\frac {A(1+{\text{ATK }}\%+{\text{TTDS Buff}})+{\text{Flat ATK}}}{A(1+{\text{ATK }}\%)+{\text{Flat ATK}}}}+{\frac {\text{Bennett Buff}}{A(1+{\text{ATK }}\%)+{\text{Flat ATK}}}}\\&={\text{TTDS ATK Increase Ratio}}+{\frac {\text{Bennett Buff}}{\text{Initial ATK}}}\end{aligned}$$
