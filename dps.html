MV to DPS

DPS numbers are expressed as % (so 2000% would be a multiplier of 20, dividing by 100%) and are normalized after considering Bennett's buff (~1.5 multiplier) assuming an ATK of 2000 for most characters. Actual dps would also consider DEF, RES, etc.

The factor to multiply the mv is $$2000*1.5*.5*.9 = 1350$$ and if there is 200% crit damage $$1350*3 = 4050$$ or roughly 4000 conversion ratio between mv and actual dps. Note in practice, some units (notably supports) may have less than 2000 atk and 200% crit damage meaning the actual dps will be less. Moreover things like res shred (VV etc) or even def shred can change the conversion ratio. It ultimately depends on each unit and team composition, but this number is given as a very rough estimate. Do not take this out of context. In practice you can do more or less damage.

Spiral Abyss

What is the minimum dps needed to clear abyss (9 stars)? Suppose floor 12 has a lv100 maguu kenki in one chamber. Floor 12 gets a x2.5 multiplier to hp. The maguu has M = 1838256 hp. Say both sides have one. We need to clear in 3 minutes or 180s. The dps needed is \(2M/180=20425\) damage per second.

Now divide by 4000. The team dps needed is 500% mv. This is very achievable but both sides need to have 500% mv average. Some characters who have >500% can even solo the floor.

In general one can probably get away with less since not every enemy is a maguu kenki. Also aoe situations can reduce the amount of mv needed if you can hit multiple enemies at once.

In practice we also care about efficiency. Can all your attacks hit? If attacks miss like ganyu's charged attack or is blocked by the maguu kenki, you get inefficiency, which lowers actual dps.
